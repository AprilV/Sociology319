The Epiphany (How This Project Started)

"Wait. WAIT. This is literally what we're studying in my sociology class right now. Sherry Turkle's 'Alone Together' - except I'm experiencing the OPPOSITE of what she describes."

So here's what happened: I've been working with GitHub Copilot (an AI coding assistant) for about 4 weeks now to build an NFL analytics app for another class. And while reading Turkle's "Alone Together" for SOC319, I had this moment where I realized‚ÄîI'm not experiencing isolation or weird attachment issues like she describes. I'm actually having a pretty normal working relationship, just... with AI.

Then I started noticing that when I talk to classmates or family about using AI, they're either scared of it ("Terminator" references, seriously), think it's cheating, or have no idea how to use it effectively. And that's when it hit me: this might be a new kind of digital divide.

Theories I'm Planning to Use (and Why)

My professor talks about using sociological theories to explain what you're observing. Here's what I think might apply to AI collaboration:

Social Exchange Theory

Why: This is about weighing costs vs. benefits in relationships. I can use it to explain why some people try AI (benefits seem worth it) and why others avoid it (costs like fear or time feel too high).

Symbolic Interactionism

Why: This theory looks at how people communicate and create shared meanings. Seems relevant because working with AI is all about figuring out how to communicate effectively.

Digital Divide Theory

Why: Obviously relevant since my whole project is about AI creating inequality. Traditional digital divide is about internet access‚Äîmine is about skills and psychological barriers.
