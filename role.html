My Role (Draft)

Individual Action and Contribution

What I'm Actually Doing

I'm not running an organization or creating policy, but I am doing something. When people ask me about AI (which happens a lot because I finish projects faster than they do), I explain it. When family makes Terminator jokes, I talk about what AI actually is and isn't. I'm documenting everything publicly‚Äîthis website, my GitHub repos, my notes. Trying to be a bridge between "AI is scary" and "AI is useful."

1. Informal Education

2. Public Documentation

3. Research and Analysis

This project itself is action. Documenting and analyzing the AI literacy divide, connecting it to sociological theory, making it visible as an inequality issue. Research is a form of contribution even if I'm not implementing solutions.

Limitations and Realistic Expectations

I'm one person. I can't fix systemic inequality. I can't create university-wide AI literacy programs or change government policy. But I can:

  • Help the people immediately around me
  • Document what I'm learning so others can learn from it
  • Contribute to the conversation about AI inequality through research
  • Model what effective AI collaboration looks like

That's not nothing, but it's also not enough to solve the problem. Individual action matters, but systemic problems need systemic solutions. I'm doing what I can while recognizing what I can't.
