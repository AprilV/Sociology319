About the Author

April V. Sykes is a 56-year-old IT professional and returning scholar who finds herself on the unexpected side of the AI literacy divide. With over twenty years of experience in IT infrastructure—including leadership roles at Dell Technologies managing hospital systems across three states—she represents a statistical anomaly: an older adult who has not only adopted AI technology but has integrated it deeply into her technical and academic work.

Currently completing her Bachelor of Applied Science in Information Systems at Olympic College, April has earned President's Scholar honors for nine consecutive terms while maintaining a 3.94 GPA. She is preparing for CAPM certification and uses AI as a collaborative partner in building applications, solving complex technical problems, and managing her academic workload. Yet when she looks around her classroom, she sees peers her age who view the same technology with fear and skepticism—a divide that both troubles and fascinates her.

This research emerges from that tension: the gap between her experience and her classmates', between the 34% who have crossed the AI literacy divide and the 66% who remain on the other side. It is an autoethnographic exploration of what made the difference, written by someone who understands both the promise of AI and the very real barriers that keep most people her age from ever trying.

Professional Background

April's technical career spans over two decades in IT infrastructure, with deep expertise in IBM mainframe systems and operations. As a System Analyst and Team Lead at Dell Technologies (contracted to CHI/Jewish Hospital and UofL Health), she managed 12-person teams supporting 5,000+ endpoints across regional hospitals in three states, achieving 100% system uptime during critical healthcare system migrations.

Her technical expertise spans IBM mainframes, Cisco networking, 24/7 operations management, and healthcare IT security compliance. Currently pursuing project management as her professional focus, she holds a Certified Associate in Project Management (CAPM) credential along with CompTIA Network+, Linux+, and A+ certifications.

Academic Credentials
