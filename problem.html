Turkle's Framework on Human-Robot Relationships

What Turkle Found: Children and Robots

Sherry Turkle's Alone Together documents how children form emotional bonds with robots and other computational objects. She studied kids interacting with AIBO (robot dog), Furby, and other "relational artifacts" - objects designed to evoke relationship-like behaviors.

My Situation: How It Compares

Differences

  • Age: I'm an adult with metacognitive awareness, not a child
  • Form: Conversational AI (text-based), not physical robot
  • Context: Work/productivity context, not play or therapy
  • Understanding: I know it's algorithms and training data, understand how it works

Similarities

  • Relational behaviors: I say "we," celebrate successes, feel frustrated
  • Trust development: Testing ‚Üí validation ‚Üí acceptance (like Turkle's children)
  • Anthropomorphism: Treating AI like collaborator despite knowing it's not sentient
  • Testing: Checking if AI "remembers" things, learns my patterns
  • Knowing vs. feeling: I KNOW it's not alive but sometimes FEEL like it understands me

Research Question This Raises

If Turkle's framework about children and robots also applies to adults and conversational AI, what does that mean? Is this:

  • The same phenomenon (human-AI relational bonds) just in different forms?
  • Something fundamentally different because I'm an adult with more awareness?
  • Evidence that Turkle's framework needs updating for conversational AI era?
  • Context-dependent (work relationships different from therapeutic/play relationships)?

The only way to answer is to document my experience systematically and compare it to what Turkle found. That's what the rest of this research does.
