The Problem (Draft)

AI Literacy as a New Digital Divide

Work in Progress - Defining the inequality I'm studying

What I Think the Problem Is

Okay so here's what I've noticed: I can work with AI pretty effectively. But when I talk to other people‚Äîclassmates, family, even people in my classes‚Äîthey either have no idea how to use AI, are scared of it, or think it's going to "take over the world." Everyone has the same access to these tools (ChatGPT is free, GitHub Copilot is available), but there's this huge gap in who actually uses them well.

"Most people I've talked to about AI are either: 1) Scared of it ('Terminator' references, job loss fears), 2) Dismissive of it ('it's just a fancy autocomplete'), or 3) Have never really engaged with it beyond ChatGPT for homework (which they feel guilty about)."
‚Äî From my notes, Week 3

So I'm calling this the "AI Literacy Divide"‚Äîit's not about having computers or internet (traditional digital divide), it's about knowing HOW to work with AI and not being too scared to try.

Why This is Different from Regular "Digital Divide"

In class we learned about digital divide as who has computers/internet vs. who doesn't. But this AI thing is different:

  • Everyone I know has access to AI tools. ChatGPT is free. GitHub Copilot is available. It's not an access problem.
  • The barrier is psychological and educational. People don't know HOW to use it, or they're too scared to try.
  • It's about skills, not equipment. Like, you can have a piano but that doesn't mean you can play it.

To research: Find academic articles about "second-level digital divide" or "skills gap" - I think that's what this is called?

Why This Matters (The "So What?")

If some people can work effectively with AI and others can't, that creates real inequality:

üíº

Jobs

Jobs that require working with AI will probably pay better. If you can't learn these skills, you're stuck with worse opportunities.

üéì

School

Students who can use AI effectively (without just copy-pasting) will do better. That affects grades, college applications, everything.

It Gets Worse Over Time

The gap grows. People who use AI get better at it and accomplish more. People who avoid it fall further behind. Classic rich-get-richer problem.
