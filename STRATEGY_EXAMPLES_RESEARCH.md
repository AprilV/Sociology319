## STRATEGY 1: EDUCATION & TRAINING PROGRAMS

**Goal:** Find ONE specific education initiative addressing AI literacy

### Potential Examples to Research:

#### Example 1A: Google's AI Essentials Certificate
- **Program:** Google Career Certificates - AI Essentials
- **Target:** General public, no prerequisites
- **Cost:** $49/month Coursera subscription
- **Content:** AI fundamentals, practical applications, ethical considerations
- **Research questions:**
  - Who completes this? (Demographics)
  - Does it improve AI adoption/comfort?
  - Cost barrier - does $49/month exclude low-income learners?
  - Completion rates?
- **Search terms:** "Google AI Essentials effectiveness," "Google AI certificate outcomes," "AI training program success rates"

#### Example 1B: Microsoft AI Skills Initiative
- **Program:** Microsoft Learn AI modules (free)
- **Target:** Developers, IT professionals, students
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Content:** Azure AI, responsible AI, practical implementation
- **Research questions:**
  - Accessibility - does it require coding background?
  - Who uses free resources vs. paid options?
  - Does free = effective, or do people need structured programs?
- **Search terms:** "Microsoft AI training effectiveness," "free AI education programs," "AI upskilling initiatives"

#### Example 1C: K-12 AI Literacy Initiatives
- **Program:** AI4K12 (AI for K-12 initiative by AAAI/CSTA)
- **Target:** Children, teachers, schools
- **Cost:** Free resources for educators
- **Content:** Age-appropriate AI concepts, curriculum guidelines
- **Research questions:**
  - Does early exposure reduce future fear/resistance?
  - Teacher adoption rates - do educators feel equipped?
  - Equity - do all schools implement, or just well-resourced districts?
- **Search terms:** "K-12 AI education," "AI4K12 implementation," "teaching AI in schools"

**PICK ONE. Evaluate:**
- ‚úÖ What works? (Evidence of success)
- ‚ùå What doesn't work? (Limitations, who's excluded)

## STRATEGY 2: POLICY & REGULATION

**Goal:** Find ONE specific policy/regulation addressing AI access or transparency

### Potential Examples to Research:

#### Example 2A: EU AI Act (2024)
- **Policy:** European Union Artificial Intelligence Act
- **Goal:** Regulate AI systems, require transparency, protect citizens
- **Key provisions:**
  - High-risk AI systems must be transparent/explainable
  - Prohibited AI uses (social scoring, manipulation)
  - Requirements for human oversight
- **Research questions:**
  - Does transparency requirement help non-technical users understand AI?
  - Does regulation INCREASE or DECREASE AI adoption?
  - Who benefits - consumers, companies, or neither?
  - Enforcement challenges?
- **Search terms:** "EU AI Act effectiveness," "AI transparency regulations," "does AI regulation help users"

#### Example 2B: US Executive Order on AI (October 2023)
- **Policy:** Biden's Executive Order on Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy AI
- **Goal:** Establish AI safety standards, promote innovation, protect rights
- **Key provisions:**
  - Safety testing requirements
  - Equity and civil rights protections
  - Support for workers affected by AI
- **Research questions:**
  - Does this address AI literacy gap or just safety?
  - Worker support - actual programs or just statements?
  - Does it help people learn to use AI, or just regulate companies?
- **Search terms:** "Biden AI executive order impact," "US AI policy effectiveness," "AI worker protection programs"

#### Example 2C: Right to Explanation (GDPR Article 22)
- **Policy:** EU GDPR provision requiring explanation of automated decisions
- **Goal:** Users can demand explanation when AI makes decisions about them
- **Key provisions:**
  - Right to know when AI is used
  - Right to human review of automated decisions
  - Must be in understandable language
- **Research questions:**
  - Do people actually request explanations?
  - Are explanations understandable to non-technical users?
  - Does this empower users or just create bureaucracy?
- **Search terms:** "GDPR right to explanation," "algorithmic transparency GDPR," "does explainability help users"

**PICK ONE. Evaluate:**
- ‚úÖ Does regulation help people understand/use AI better?
- ‚ùå Does it create barriers or confusion?
- üìä Evidence of impact on AI literacy/adoption?

## STRATEGY 3: TECHNOLOGY SOLUTIONS

**Goal:** Find ONE tech solution designed to make AI more accessible/understandable

### Potential Examples to Research:

#### Example 3A: Explainable AI (XAI) Tools
- **Example:** IBM Watson OpenScale, Google's Explainable AI
- **Goal:** Make AI decisions transparent and understandable
- **How it works:** Visualizations, plain-language explanations of AI reasoning
- **Research questions:**
  - Do non-technical users understand XAI outputs?
  - Does transparency increase trust and adoption?
  - Who uses these tools - experts only, or general public?
  - Cost barrier - are XAI tools accessible?
- **Search terms:** "explainable AI effectiveness," "does XAI increase trust," "accessible AI explanations"

#### Example 3B: Conversational AI with Natural Language
- **Example:** ChatGPT's interface design, Claude, accessible AI chatbots
- **Goal:** Lower barrier to entry - talk to AI naturally, no coding required
- **How it works:** Natural language prompts, user-friendly interfaces
- **Research questions:**
  - Does conversational interface reduce fear/intimidation?
  - Do non-technical users adopt conversational AI more than traditional tools?
  - Quality of outputs - do beginners get hallucinations/errors?
  - Digital divide - still requires internet, device, literacy
- **Search terms:** "conversational AI adoption," "ChatGPT accessibility," "natural language AI interfaces"

#### Example 3C: Low-Code/No-Code AI Platforms
- **Example:** Google AutoML, Microsoft Power Platform AI Builder
- **Goal:** Let non-programmers build/use AI without coding
- **How it works:** Drag-and-drop interfaces, pre-built models, templates
- **Research questions:**
  - Who actually uses these - true beginners, or still tech-savvy users?
  - Quality of results - do they work well enough?
  - Cost - many charge enterprise pricing, excluding individuals
  - Do they democratize AI or just shift who the experts are?
- **Search terms:** "no-code AI platforms," "AutoML accessibility," "democratizing AI development"

**PICK ONE. Evaluate:**
- ‚úÖ Does better UX actually solve literacy problem?
- ‚ùå Who's still excluded (cost, digital access, language)?
- üìä Evidence of adoption by non-technical users?

## EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

### Use This to Assess Each Strategy:

**Effectiveness:**
- Does it measurably improve AI literacy/adoption?
- Is there data showing impact?
- What does success look like for this strategy?

**Accessibility:**
- Who can access this strategy?
- Who's excluded? (Cost, language, tech requirements, geography)
- Does it address the digital divide or reinforce it?

**Scalability:**
- Can this approach reach large numbers of people?
- Is it sustainable long-term?
- Resource requirements?

**Equity:**
- Does it reduce inequality or create new gaps?
- Who benefits most?
- Does it address root causes or just symptoms?
