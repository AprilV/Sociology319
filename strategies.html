Assignment Requirement - Framework set up, specific examples to be researched

My plan: Look at education, policy, and technology solutions. They seem different enough to count as three separate strategies.

Strategy 2: Policy and Regulation

Basically: Government rules about how AI should work

The Idea

Make laws/policies requiring AI to be transparent and explainable. If people understand how AI works, maybe they'll be less scared and more willing to learn.

Examples to Find

Need to research: EU AI Act (heard about this in class), explainability laws, worker protection rules around AI/automation

Pros

Could protect people from harmful AI. Makes companies responsible. Creates baseline standards.

Cons

Hard to enforce. Might slow down helpful innovation. Different countries have different rules. Doesn't actually teach people skills.

My take: Policy might reduce fear (if AI is regulated it seems less scary?) but won't teach people how to use it well. Need both?

Strategy 3: Better Technology Design

Make AI tools easier to use so more people can access them

The Idea

Design AI with better interfaces, clearer explanations of what it's doing, and less technical jargon. Meet people where they are instead of expecting them to become experts.

Examples to Find

Need to research: "Explainable AI" tools, no-code AI platforms, user-friendly AI assistants designed for non-technical people

Pros

Can be deployed quickly. Helps immediately. Reduces intimidation factor.

Cons

Doesn't address fear. Oversimplifying might hide important details. Still requires internet/devices. Companies control it.

My take: GitHub Copilot isn't super user-friendly (you need to know coding). A better interface might help, but my classmates' problem is fear, not complexity.

Comparing the Three

Honestly, none of these alone seems like enough. You probably need all three:

  • Education teaches skills (long-term)
  • Policy protects people and builds trust (structural)
  • Technology makes it easier to get started (immediate)
