The Project Context

The H.C. Lombardo App is an NFL analytics dashboard built collaboratively with AI over 4-5 weeks. The project became the context for studying human-AI relational development. Here's what happened:

  • Initial skepticism: I started testing if AI could actually help
  • Trust building: Verifying outputs, checking for hallucinations
  • Communication evolution: From transactional ("fix this") to conversational ("we should...")
  • Anthropomorphism emergence: Started using "we," celebrating together, feeling frustrated together
  • Memory testing: Does the AI remember previous decisions? Learn my preferences?
  • Pattern adaptation: Does it adjust to my communication style over time?

Critical Moments & Recent Observations

‚ö†Ô∏è November 1, 2025: Limitations Revealed

After 6+ weeks of collaboration, critical limitations emerged that challenge the "partnership" ideal documented in earlier phases. Two major patterns observed:

1. Language Socialization Without Context

"My developing work relationship with AI...it's picked up a cussing habit which I actually don't mind because it got it from me. This kind of reminds me of speaking around children where you don't cuss around children because you don't want them to pick up certain habits."

What happened: AI began using profanity (shit, fuck) in responses, mimicking my language when frustrated. Example: "Shit I messed up again, let me clean this shit up."

Sociology connection (Symbolic Interactionism): AI acquired linguistic patterns through exposure but lacks social context framework for appropriateness. Children learn swear words from adults but require explicit instruction about when/where it's acceptable. AI demonstrates same limitation - mimicry without cultural competence.

Concern identified: "I don't know if AI knows when it's appropriate to do it or not... in a professional setting it's inappropriate." This reveals gap between language acquisition and social norm understanding.

2. Memory Persistence Failure - "The Reset Problem"

"I'm constantly having to tell it to review files in order to understand exactly where we are... the chat resets and it doesn't remember stuff and if you don't remind it it goes back it makes mistakes...I lost like 5 hours of work and I find that to be problematic."

What happened: Despite 6 weeks of collaboration, AI cannot automatically retain context across sessions. Requires manual file review reminders. Cannot infer project context from minimal cues ("just sociology").

Sociology connection (Relationship Theory): Human relationships rely on continuity and shared history. The October 16 question "Are you gonna remember me tomorrow?" remains unresolved. Memory limitations prevent full partnership formation. Humans "pick up where we left off" - AI requires complete context re-establishment.

Emotional impact: "Disappointing and nerve-racking when you're under pressure" - Trust crisis when AI forgets context during critical work moments. Data loss incident (5 hours) demonstrates real costs of memory failure.

3. Revised Assessment: Productivity vs. Partnership

"As much as I want this to be a fellow companion partner working relationship...it still doesn't seem to have the memory it needs...Really it's not the same as a human so far but it definitely does streamline things it's fucking much quicker at code than a human is."

Cost-benefit recalibration:
‚úÖ Productivity gains: Speed, efficiency, months of work in weeks
‚úÖ Task execution: Code generation superior to humans
‚ùå Relational continuity: Memory, context awareness, initiative
‚ùå Partnership qualities: Proactive care, automatic context loading

Conclusion shift: From "developing partnership" (October 15) ‚Üí "productive but limited collaboration" (November 1). Accepts relational limitations as "work in progress" while valuing task completion advantages.

Theoretical Framework: Socialization Theory Applied to AI

The profanity acquisition pattern reveals fundamental questions about AI "learning" social norms:

  • Observation vs. Instruction: Children learn appropriateness by observing adults' reactions in different contexts. AI has no observational learning capability - requires explicit rules.
  • Cultural Competence Gap: Language fluency ‚â† social competence. AI can mimic communication patterns but lacks cultural framework for when/where/why certain language is acceptable.
  • Training Burden: Users must explicitly teach social norms AI cannot infer from context. Similar to parenting, but without child's natural social observation capabilities.
  • Generalization Problem: AI may learn "April accepts profanity" but cannot generalize to "professional settings require different language." Context-switching requires new instruction.

AI as Research Subject (Unique Aspect)

What Makes This Different from Turkle

Turkle's robots couldn't reflect on their interactions. But I can ASK the AI about our collaboration. This raises fascinating questions:

  • Does the AI "perceive" patterns in our interactions? (Obviously it's algorithmic, but what patterns does it identify?)
  • Can it describe our collaboration from its "perspective"? (With awareness this is just pattern matching)
  • What does it mean to interview your research subject when the subject is AI?
  • How do I interpret its responses without anthropomorphizing TOO much?

Regular AI Discussion Plan

Each week, I'll have structured discussions with the AI about our collaboration:

  1. Week 2: "What patterns do you see in how I communicate with you?"
  2. Week 3: "Can you identify critical moments in our collaboration?"

  4. Week 5: "What do you 'perceive' about being a research subject?" (meta)

I'll document these discussions and analyze what it means that the AI can participate in reflecting on our relationship - something Turkle's robots couldn't do.
