Our Collaboration: The Divide in Action

6 Weeks of AI Partnership Building the H.C. Lombardo App

The Divide Made Personal

Over six weeks, I built a complex NFL analytics dashboardthe H.C. Lombardo Appusing AI as my primary development partner. The app features real-time NFL data, interactive dashboards, multiple data sources, and advanced analytics. It represents months of traditional development work compressed into weeks through AI collaboration.

But this research isn't about the app itself. It's about what happened when I told my classmates about it.

Two Sides of the Divide

My Experience

  • Built complex app in 6 weeks
  • Documented 2,700+ lines of observations
  • 100+ Git commits with AI
  • Learned Python, databases, APIs
  • See AI as productivity amplifier
  • Comfortable experimenting, iterating
  • Understand AI limitations clearly

\"I've been using AI since ChatGPT launched in 2022. This is normal for me.\"

My Classmates' Reactions

  • \"Isn't that cheating?\"
  • \"AI is going to replace us all\"
  • \"That's scary\" / \"Terminator\"
  • \"I don't trust it\"
  • \"What if it makes a mistake?\"
  • Refuse to try ChatGPT
  • Fear admitting they don't understand

\"They fear what they don't understand. That's the divide.\"

The gap isn't technicalit's psychological. My classmates have the same access to ChatGPT that I do. They have computers. They have internet. But they lack the willingness to experiment, the trust to begin, and the framework to understand what AI can and cannot do.

My 6-Week Journey: From Skeptic to Builder

Phase 1: Testing & Verification (Weeks 1-2)

What I did: Started with small tasks. Verified every output. Checked for \"hallucinations\" (AI making things up). Explicit instructions: \"Make sure you backup first,\" \"Don't forget to commit to Git.\"

Trust level: Low. I treated AI like an intern who might make mistakes.

Phase 2: Pattern Recognition (Weeks 3-4)

What I did: AI started anticipating my needsproactively suggesting backups, remembering project conventions, learning my communication style. I needed fewer explicit reminders.

Trust level: Growing. I started delegating more complex tasks.

Phase 3: Partnership (Weeks 5-6)

What I did: Communication evolved from step-by-step instructions (\"Make sure you backup, then commit, then push to Git\") to shorthand (\"prod, backup, push\"). I started saying \"we\" instead of \"I.\" Example: \"We need to fix this dashboard issue.\"

Trust level: High, but realistic. I understand AI's capabilities and its limitations.

Reality Check: AI's Limitations

AI doesn't have perfect memory across sessions. It can forget context if not reminded. It can make mistakes. I lost 5 hours of work once when AI misunderstood the project state. But here's the key difference: I learned from that. I adapted my workflow. My classmates never got past the fear of trying.

What This Collaboration Demonstrates

1. AI Literacy Is a Skill, Not a Gift

I'm not inherently \"good with technology.\" I'm 56 years old. I learned through experimentation, patience, and willingness to fail. My classmates could do the samebut fear stops them before they start.

2. The Divide Is Self-Perpetuating

Every week I don't use AI, I fall further behind those who do. My 6 weeks of experience compoundbetter prompts, faster workflows, deeper understanding. Meanwhile, non-users' fear grows as AI advances. The gap widens exponentially.

3. Understanding Limitations Builds Trust

My classmates fear AI will \"take over\" or \"replace everyone.\" I know AI forgets context, hallucinates data, and needs constant verification. Realistic understanding reduces fear. Ignorance amplifies it.

4. Access Adoption

Everyone in my class has free access to ChatGPT. The 66% of Americans who haven't used it aren't blocked by paywalls or technologythey're blocked by fear, lack of knowledge, and absence of trusted guides. This is a cultural and educational problem, not a technological one.

The Critical Question

If I can learn AI collaboration in 6 weeks at age 56, why can't everyone? What barrierspsychological, educational, culturalprevent the 66% from even trying?
