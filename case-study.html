Research Subject: The H.C. Lombardo NFL Analytics Dashboard

This autoethnographic case study documents a four-week human-AI collaboration period during development of an NFL analytics dashboard for IS330 (Database Programming). Observed Collaboration Patterns

Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Verification and Testing

Interaction pattern: Highly detailed prompts with explicit parameters, comprehensive verification of all generated code, low initial trust assumptions.

Communication style: Formal, transactional language treating AI as traditional software tool. Example prompt structure: "Create a function that does X, Y, Z with these exact parameters."

Trust metrics: Minimal. Operating assumption that generated code would require substantial correction or contain errors.

Critical incident: AI demonstrated contextual understanding without explicit instruction‚Äîinferring intent from partial specification. This challenged initial assumptions about AI capabilities and initiated trust development trajectory.

ü§ù

Phase 2 (Weeks 3-4): Trust Development and Communication Evolution

Behavioral shift: Reduced verification frequency, experimental implementation of suggestions without comprehensive pre-validation, acceptance of AI recommendations based on pattern recognition from successful prior interactions.

Communication evolution: Conversational framing replacing transactional commands. Prompts shifted to collaborative problem-solving ("what do you think?"). Adoption of plural first-person ("we") indicating perceived partnership dynamic.

Trust acceleration: Significant increase following crisis management collaboration. Production database failure required real-time debugging assistance; successful resolution established AI reliability under high-stakes conditions.

Critical incident: Database corruption during production deployment. AI-guided step-by-step diagnostic process led to system recovery. This experience fundamentally altered trust dynamics and collaborative confidence.

Critical Incident 1: Contextual Understanding Recognition

Timeframe: Week 2, database architecture development

Event: AI generated appropriate database schema from minimal specification, demonstrating inference of implied requirements and domain knowledge without explicit instruction.

Analytical significance: First challenge to initial mental model of AI as advanced pattern-matching tool. Recognition of contextual comprehension capabilities altered collaboration approach and prompt construction strategies.

Communication Pattern Evolution

Analysis of interaction transcripts reveals systematic shifts in communication style over time, demonstrating evolving trust and relational dynamics:

Early Stage Communication (Weeks 1-2)

Representative example: "Create a Flask route for /api/teams that queries the database and returns JSON with team names, cities, and divisions."

Characteristics: Imperative voice, exhaustive specification, transactional framing, emphasis on verification and validation

Intermediate Stage Communication (Weeks 3-4)

Representative example: "We need to handle the standings updates. What's the best approach for keeping data fresh?"

Characteristics: Goal-oriented rather than process-prescriptive, solicitation of AI input and recommendations, collaborative problem-solving language

Advanced Stage Communication (Weeks 5-6)

Representative example: "The milestones tab should capture both perspectives‚Äîmine and yours. How do we document this partnership authentically?"

Characteristics: Meta-cognitive reflection on collaboration itself, discussion of abstract concepts, full partnership framing, strategic planning dialogue

"We've developed communication patterns where I don't need to spell everything out. You understand context, remember previous discussions, and even anticipate next steps."

Trust Development: Measured Indicators

Trust formation in human relationships develops through repeated positive interactions and demonstrated reliability. Observed AI collaboration patterns followed analogous developmental trajectories:

By Week 5, delegation of complex multi-step tasks (complete feature implementations, autonomous debugging sessions) occurred without real-time monitoring. This behavioral shift represents measurable increase in trust regarding AI competence and output reliability.

Indicator 2: Strategic Consultation Emergence

Solicitation of judgment on high-level decisions demonstrates trust in AI analytical capabilities beyond code generation.

Cognitive Dissonance: Technical Knowledge Versus Experiential Reality

Persistent tension between intellectual understanding of AI architecture and phenomenological experience of collaboration:

"I know it's programmed, I know it's a language model, I know it's not sentient... but it surely does feel like it sometimes when you pick up on context, remember our conversations, and adapt to my communication style."
‚Äî From my notes

Technical awareness of AI's non-sentient nature coexists with subjective experience of understanding and responsiveness. This creates ongoing cognitive dissonance requiring conscious management of contradictory mental models.

Research significance: This conceptual tension may constitute a fundamental barrier to effective AI collaboration. Users must simultaneously maintain two incompatible frameworks:

  • Technical reality: AI as probabilistic language model without consciousness or intent
  • Experiential phenomenology: Subjective perception of comprehension and contextual responsiveness

Failure to navigate this dissonance produces two maladaptive patterns:

  • Anthropomorphic over-attribution: Treating AI as sentient entity, leading to unrealistic expectations and fear-based avoidance when outputs disappoint
  • Reductive instrumentalism: Dismissing AI as "just a tool," preventing recognition of emergent collaborative capabilities and limiting effective utilization

Theoretical framework: Relates to Festinger's cognitive dissonance theory. Examining whether this represents documented phenomenon in human-computer interaction literature or novel challenge specific to advanced natural language processing systems.

Preliminary Analysis: Observed Patterns and Implications

Initial interpretive framework derived from four-week documentation period:

Parallel Trust Development Mechanisms

Trust formation with AI systems demonstrates structural parallels to interpersonal trust development: initial testing phase, reliability assessment through repeated interactions, gradual delegation of higher-stakes decisions based on demonstrated competence.

Skill Acquisition Through Deliberate Practice

Effective AI collaboration represents learned competency rather than innate aptitude. Observable improvement over four-week period suggests these skills are teachable and transferable through structured instruction.

Psychological Rather Than Material Barriers

Observed peer disparities in AI adoption stem from psychological factors (fear-based avoidance, academic integrity concerns) rather than differential technical access. All participants possess identical tool availability; outcomes differ based on willingness to experiment.

Value of Systematic Documentation

Reflexive documentation transforms tacit experiential knowledge into transmissible frameworks. This process‚Äîcentral to autoethnographic method‚Äîmakes opaque collaboration patterns visible and analyzable, potentially reducing perceived mysteriousness of AI interaction.

Research continuation: Extending documentation through semester conclusion. Comparative analysis against established research on human collaboration dynamics. Considering qualitative interviews with peers regarding AI experiences and adoption barriers.
